# Vehicle Type Analysis

This folder focuses on analyzing different vehicle types involved in NYC motor vehicle collisions.  
The aim was to identify which vehicle types are most frequently involved in crashes and which ones have higher fatality rates.

## üöó Which Vehicle Types Are Most Frequently Involved
![Which Vehicle Types Are Most Frequently Involved](./Which%20vehicle%20types%20are%20most%20frequently%20involved.png)

**Insights:**
- **Sedans** are involved in the highest number of crashes, crossing **640K** incidents.  
- **Station Wagons/SUVs** come next, followed by **Passenger Vehicles**.  
- **Taxis** show the lowest crash count among the listed types.  
- The trend suggests that the most common personal-use vehicles (like sedans and SUVs) naturally have more crash involvement due to their higher numbers on the road.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Vehicle Types: Total Crashes vs Fatality Rate
![Vehicle Types: Total Crashes vs Fatality Rate](./Vehicle%20Types_%20Total%20Crashes%20vs%20Fatality%20Rate.png)

**Insights:**
- While **Sedans** lead in total crashes, **Motorcycles** have a much higher **fatality rate (over 2.5%)**, making them the riskiest type of vehicle per crash.  
- SUVs and Passenger Vehicles have moderate fatality rates despite high crash counts.  
- The sharp fatality spike for motorcycles highlights the vulnerability of riders in urban traffic.

---

### üß≠ Summary
- Sedans and SUVs dominate crash counts, reflecting their common use in NYC.  
- Motorcycles, though less frequent in crashes, are **far more dangerous** in terms of fatal outcomes.  
- These findings can help guide targeted road safety measures, such as improving motorcycle awareness campaigns and enforcing safety gear use.
