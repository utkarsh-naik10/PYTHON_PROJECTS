# Vehicle Type & Premise Analysis (2020 to Present)

This folder explores crime patterns related to **vehicles** and **premises** in Los Angeles from 2020 to the present.  
It focuses on when, where, and how vehicle-related crimes occur and which premises are most affected.

---

## 1️⃣ Crimes Related to Vehicles

![Crimes Related to Vehicles](Crimes%20Related%20to%20Vehicles.png)

**Insights:**
- **Vehicle theft** is by far the most common, with over **115K cases**, followed by **burglary from vehicle** and **theft from motor vehicles**.
- Together, these account for more than 80% of all vehicle-related crimes.
- Attempted thefts and other minor categories (like throwing objects at vehicles) are comparatively rare.

---

## 2️⃣ Common Time and Areas for Vehicle Crimes

![Common Time and Areas for Vehicle Crimes](Common%20Time%20and%20Areas%20for%20Vehicle%20Crimes.png)

**Insights:**
- Crimes peak during the **Evening Rush (18–22 hrs)** across nearly all areas.
- **Pacific**, **Central**, and **Newton** divisions show the highest numbers, especially in evening and night hours.
- Lower counts are seen in the **Morning Rush**, when public movement is higher and visibility better.

---

## 3️⃣ Monthly Trend of Vehicle Thefts or Break-ins

![Monthly Trend of Vehicle Thefts or Break-ins](Monthly%20Trend%20of%20Vehicle%20Thefts%20or%20Break-ins.png)

**Insights:**
- Vehicle thefts rise during **summer months (June–October)**, peaking around **October**.
- Burglary from vehicles follows a similar pattern but stays more consistent through the year.
- Both metrics dip around **February** and **December**, possibly due to weather or holidays.

---

## 4️⃣ Premise-wise Crime Frequency by Year

![Premise-wise Crime Frequency by Year](Premise-wise%20Crime%20Frequency%20by%20Year.png)

**Insights:**
- **Streets, single-family dwellings, and multi-unit dwellings** see the most consistent crime activity every year.
- Crimes in **parking lots** and **business premises** remain stable, while public spots like **parks or schools** have negligible cases.
- There’s a small decline in most premises during **2024–2025**, possibly due to incomplete yearly data.

---

## 5️⃣ Top 10 Premises Where Crimes Occur

![Top 10 Premises Where Crimes Occur](Top%2010%20Premises%20Where%20Crimes%20Occur.png)

**Insights:**
- The **street** is the top location for crimes (over 260K incidents), followed by **homes** (single-family and multi-unit dwellings).
- **Parking lots** and **business areas** also rank high, showing that both private and public spaces are vulnerable.
- Indoor or secured premises (like garages or stores) have far fewer crimes.

---

## 6️⃣ Victim Profile in Vehicle-Related Crimes

![Victim Profile in Vehicle-Related Crimes](Victim%20Profile%20in%20Vehicle-Related%20Crimes.png)

**Insights:**
- Over **54% of victims** have **unknown gender**, likely due to incomplete records for vehicle owners.
- Among known victims, **males (27%)** and **females (19%)** show similar proportions.
- Most victims are **adults (30–44 yrs)**, followed by **young (18–29)**, showing vehicle ownership age range.
- In descent distribution, **White** and **Hispanic/Latin** victims are nearly equal in number, dominating the dataset.

---

## 7️⃣ Weapon Involvement in Vehicle Crimes

![Weapon Involvement in Vehicle Crimes](Weapon%20Involvement%20in%20Vehicle%20Crimes.png)

**Insights:**
- In over **260K cases**, the **weapon is unknown**, confirming that most vehicle crimes are **non-violent property crimes**.
- Among known cases, **bodily force**, **verbal threats**, and **handguns** appear occasionally.
- Overall, violence in vehicle-related crimes is extremely rare — they’re largely theft or burglary cases.

---

## 8️⃣ Area vs Premise Relationship

![Area vs Premise Relationship](Area%20vs%20Premise%20Relationship.png)

**Insights:**
- **Pacific, Newton, and Southwest** divisions report high crime counts on **streets and dwellings**.
- **Hollywood and Olympic** areas show more incidents around **businesses and apartments**.
- The heatmap highlights how urban density correlates with both vehicle and premise-related incidents.

| Category | Observation |
